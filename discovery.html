Buddhify website

As is analysis

I analyzed the strengths and weaknesses of the website, taking into account its core values and objectives, and reviewed both desktop and mobile versions to assess responsiveness.

I then evaluated the site against Jakob Nielsen’s 10 usability heuristics and mapped the current information architecture with brief descriptions of each section.

This process helped to rationalize the content and laid the foundation for revisiting and reorganizing the information architecture in the following phase.

Buddhify website

User research and competitor analysis

In analyzing the user research, I considered the competitors of the meditation app Buddhify—for example, the community-driven approach of Insight Timer, the science-based design of Headspace, the personalization features of Calm, and the emotional depth of Meditopia.

From these, I evaluated specific features such as responsive design, login pages, free subscription options, user reviews, customer support, FAQs, and more.

I then analyzed Buddhify’s target audience, taking into account the app’s values and purpose.

Through a survey, I gathered insights that led to key conclusions, which were later used to inform improvements to the app.
